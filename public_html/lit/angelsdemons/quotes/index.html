Angels & Demons Quotations

Science v. Religion

“Science and religion are not in this together! We do not seek the same God, you and I! Who is your God? One of protons, masses, and particle charges? How does your God inspire? How does your God reach into the hearts of man and remind him he is accountable to a greater power! Remind him that he is accountable to his fellow man! Vetra was misguided. His work was not religious, it was sacrilegious! Man cannot put God's Creation in a test tube and wave it around for the world to see! This does not glorify God, it demeans God!” — Camerlengo Carlo Ventresca

This quote clearly connects the theme of science versus religion to the novel, as this quote exemplifies the constant struggle between science and religion, and how they can seldom seem to get along. Ventresca's vicious opinion regarding science only serves as an example of one side of the spectrum in terms of how the two sides feel about the other, although it does not necessarily have to be this way.

Good v. Evil

“Those who believe undergo great tests for God! God asked Abraham to sacrifice his child! God commanded Jesus to endure crucifixion! And so we hang the symbol of the crucifix before our eyes - bloody, painful, agonizing to remind us of evil's power! To keep our hearts vigilant! The scars on Jesus' body are a living reminder of the powers of darkness! My scars are a living reminder! Evil lives, but the power of God will overcome!” — Camerlengo Carlo Ventresca

This quote serves as an example of the struggle between good and evil as Ventresca's behavior demonstrates how the church would be perceived by the public or the majority as a positive influence in the plot of the book, and how the church might try to resolve the issue, when in reality, it is one of their own that has caused the entire issue.

Morality v. Sin

“There is no risk when God is at your side. This cause was His.” — Camerlengo Carlo Ventresca

Ventresca's opposing viewpoints between his actions as serving God's word and acts of sin are demonstrating within this quote, as when Ventresca was questioned about his motives, he says that his motivation was the message of God, and that he was acting on behalf of God, while overlooking the severe level of atrocities and sin that he had committed.

Modesty v. Pride

“But I do know that until I get some answers that make sense, there is no way I will raise any sort of alarm. Vigilance and discretion are my duty… such that spiritual matters can take place here with clarity of mind.” — Commander Olivetti

Olivetti's stubborn persistence upon not acting on the issue of the antimatter in the Vatican is part of his pretentious (and eventually incorrect) mindset that the Swiss Guard was incorruptible, and his pride prevented him from detecting the urgency of the situation at hand, when action could have been taken earlier on the issue, and possibly saving some of the executed Cardinals' lives.

Public v. Private

“We all benefit from a sense of contact with divinity… even if it is only imagined.” — Camerlengo Carlo Ventresca

This final quote is an example and representation of the internal reasoning of the church hierarchy regarding their decisions to make public certain information relating to the antimatter crisis in the Vatican. This quote shows Ventresca's willing to bend the minds of the people through manipulation to convince them of a certain fact, and this type of mindset is directly correlational to the decisions by the Catholic Church to keep this issue a secret (that is, until the Hassasin attracted the attention of the media to the issue).
